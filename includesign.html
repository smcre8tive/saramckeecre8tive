UX Design — Case Study 01

IncluDesign

A multi-product ecosystem that builds empathy through an immersive visual-impairment simulation projector game — designed for Gen-Z design students.

Role

Product Designer

Team

3 Designers

Duration

3 Weeks

Tools

Figma · FigJam · Canva

Context

SCAD Graduate — 2024

Scroll
01
Background

The Challenge

During a SCAD graduate-level class in 2024, I had the opportunity to design an immersive environment product with two other graduate teammates. We chose to focus on accessible education design — a topic we all cared about deeply.

Gen-Z design students value accessible design, but lack engaging ways to learn how to apply it effectively in their work. Theory alone wasn't cutting it.

How might we help Gen-Z design students learn accessible design in engaging ways using immersive environment technology?

02
Discovery

Research & Insights

To clarify user needs, inclusive design definitions, and market context, we used Figma and FigJam to collect secondary research and market data. After using Microsoft's Inclusive Design kit on a previous project, I suggested we adopt its framework and persona spectrum — my teammates agreed.

Microsoft Inclusive Design's methodology is born out of digital environments and draws on the full range of human diversity — including and learning from people with a range of perspectives.

We conducted primary research with SCAD design students using a Google survey and interviewed Walker, SCAD's Inclusion Director, to surface pain points that would guide our direction.

44% of design students rated their inclusive and accessibility design knowledge at a 2/5, emphasizing a gap in practical application.

"When I think of inclusion, I think of family. How do we ensure every family member is involved in the process of fulfilling or engaging in an action in the most purposeful way to achieve our goal?"

— Director Walker, SCAD Inclusion Director

Our research surfaced four core user needs — each one became a design constraint:

Empathy-Building
Students need immersive ways to understand accessibility challenges firsthand.
Practical Application
There's a gap between theory and hands-on inclusive design practice.
Accessible Tools
Learners need easy-to-use, financially accessible resources to apply inclusive principles.
Measurable Outcomes
Educators want ways to track progress in accessibility learning.
03
Process

Ideation & Iteration

Low-fidelity sketches and wireframes helped us ideate through multiple versions of our solution. We explored a responsive web app with an AR environment scanner, then pivoted to an interactive projector game when user feedback highlighted the need for a more immersive experience.

Initial sketches explored a responsive web-app with AR scanner alongside an immersive projector game concept.

After the pivot, we integrated the web-app into an ecosystem — it became a controller and accessibility support tool for the projector experience.

User flow mapping the game sequence and how players transition between screens.

Mid-fidelity wireframes using Figma's Apple Oculus design system as a foundation — adapted for the large-format projector context.

04
High Fidelity

Visual Design

We divided the high-fidelity design work across the team. I focused on the first two sections of the game — the login sequence and the spinner — while my teammates handled the design system and final game screens.

Login & Intro Sequence

Inspired by large-format interface design, I created an intro and login sequence. I identified the opportunity to use the web-app as a controller — adding a hand holding a matching mobile screen to each prototype screen to simulate the full ecosystem.

Spinner — Game Mechanic

To increase engagement, I used Figma's prototype animation features to bring the experience alive with a simulation spinner — players spin to land on a visual impairment type before the quiz begins.

Visual Impairment Simulation Quiz

Using imagery from the Sight Center of Northwest Ohio, I prototyped a visual impairment simulation quiz. Future goals include adding immersive video and sound.

05
Testing

Prototyping & Testing

We combined our designs into a high-fidelity interactive Figma prototype to simulate the projector game. We used Canva to present to SCAD design students and faculty for usability feedback.

AutoCAD architectural rendering by a teammate — showing the projector game in a real university environment, with diverse users including someone in a wheelchair.

Slides from our class presentation — representing the full team's work across research, design system, and the projector game prototype.

Class feedback highlighted the engaging nature of the game but surfaced areas for improvement — particularly around explaining the ecosystem and adding more diverse simulation scenarios. We ideated four future goals:

  • Add immersive video and sound to enhance the simulation experience.
  • Incorporate a wider range of visual impairments and scenarios.
  • Test the prototype with diverse users across the accessibility needs spectrum.
  • Source an interactive projector for real-world game design testing.
06
Outcomes

Results & Impact

Completed in a three-week academic quarter, IncluDesign was well-received by peers and faculty alike. The projector game concept demonstrated a novel approach to teaching inclusive design principles through immersive technology.

Empathy through Experience
The immersive game effectively fostered genuine empathy among design students.
Engagement in Learning
Gamification elements increased student motivation to explore accessibility.
Future Potential
Highlighted avenues for immersive tech in design education — worthy of further development.

Personally, this project deepened my understanding of remote collaboration, inclusive design principles, interactive projector affordances, and creative prototype animation. I came away with skills and perspectives I bring to every project since.
