IncluDesign Immersive Projector Game & Learning Ecosystem

Multi-product ecosystem for design students builds empathy through a visual-imparment simulaiton immersive projector game.

Role: Product Designer | Team: 3 Designers | Duration: 3 weeks | Tools: Figma, Figjam, Canva

Detail related to this project hero image

Challenge & Problem

During a SCAD graduate level class in 2024, I had the opportunity to design an immersive environment product with a cross-collaborative team. We decided to focus on accessible education design concerns for students.

Gen-Z design students value accessible design but lack engaging ways to learn how to apply it effectively. How might we help Gen-Z design students learn accessible design in engaging ways with immersive environment technology?

Research & Discovery

The initial phase focused on understanding user pain points and identifying opportunities for improvement. We conducted user interviews with 25 participants, analyzed competitor applications, and examined existing usage analytics to establish a baseline understanding of current user behavior.

Key insights from user interviews and competitive analysis highlighting main pain points
Moderated interview sessions with participants discussing their banking app experiences
Usage analytics showing drop-off points and feature engagement metrics

Our research revealed that users struggled most with complex navigation structures and lacked visibility into their spending patterns. These insights became the foundation for our design strategy moving forward.

User Personas

Based on our research findings, we developed three primary user personas representing our target audience segments. These personas helped the team maintain focus on real user needs throughout the design process.

Three primary personas representing distinct user segments and their unique needs
Journey maps for each persona showing touchpoints and emotional states
Empathy maps detailing what users think, feel, say, and do throughout their banking experience

Information Architecture

We restructured the app's information architecture to create clearer pathways to key features. The new structure reduced navigation depth from five levels to three, making essential functions more accessible.

Revised information architecture showing simplified navigation hierarchy
Card sorting exercise results revealing user mental models and content groupings
Before and after site maps demonstrating simplified structure and reduced complexity

This streamlined architecture was validated through card sorting exercises with 15 users, ensuring the new structure aligned with user mental models.

Wireframes & Iteration

Low-fidelity wireframes allowed us to rapidly test different layout concepts and interaction patterns. We created over 40 wireframe variations and conducted guerrilla testing sessions to identify the most intuitive designs.

Evolution of key screens from initial sketches through refined wireframes
Early concept sketches exploring different approaches to the dashboard layout
Detailed user flows mapping the complete journey for primary tasks
Annotated wireframes with interaction notes and responsive behavior specifications

Visual Design

The visual design phase brought the wireframes to life with a modern, accessible color palette and refined typography. We prioritized readability and ensured all color combinations met WCAG AA accessibility standards.

High-fidelity mockups showcasing the final visual design system and key screens
Comprehensive component library including buttons, forms, cards, and navigation elements
Brand colors, typography scale, and spacing system ensuring consistency across platforms
Responsive design adaptations across mobile, tablet, and desktop viewports

The design system included a comprehensive component library to ensure consistency across all touchpoints and facilitate future scalability.

Prototyping & Testing

Interactive prototypes enabled us to test the complete user experience before development. We conducted moderated usability testing with 20 participants, measuring task completion rates and gathering qualitative feedback.

Key user flows tested in the interactive prototype with success metrics
Participant completing tasks during moderated usability testing session
Interaction heatmaps revealing user attention patterns and click behaviors
Summary of usability testing findings with task completion rates and pain points

Results & Impact

Following the launch of the redesigned application, we measured significant improvements across key metrics. Task completion time decreased by 35%, and user satisfaction scores increased from 3.2 to 4.5 out of 5.

Key performance indicators showing improvements in user engagement and satisfaction
Side-by-side comparison of the old and new interfaces highlighting key improvements
Selected user feedback and testimonials from post-launch surveys
Month-over-month analytics showing sustained improvement in engagement and adoption

The redesign also led to a 28% increase in feature adoption and a 40% reduction in support tickets related to navigation confusion. These results validated our user-centered design approach and provided valuable insights for future iterations.
