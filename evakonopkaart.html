Eva Konopka Art Sales Website Redesign

Redesigning an artist's website to enhance user experience and boost art sales.

Role: UX Designer Freelance | Duration: 4 months | Tools: Google Suite, Figma, Squarespace, Canva

Eva's art shop re-design helped her sell her first artwork online.

Challenge & Problem

Eva Konopka, a talented local artist, sought to expand her art sales by creating an online presence. Her existing website was in a blog format and lacked user-friendly art sales features, making it difficult for potential buyers to navigate and purchase artwork. The challenge was to redesign the website to enhance user experience, improve navigation, and ultimately increase art sales.

To help guide our project work, I set up a project scope and timeline.

Research & Discovery

To understand the needs of Eva's users, I started with a combination of secondary research, direct obserservaiton of her art market booth, informal interviews with potential buyers. I also analyzed competitor art sales websites to identify best practices and common pain points.

Secondary research inculded website value comparisons where I helped Eva decide to stick with Squarespace instead of starting with a new website host.
Competitive Analysis of local artists and their websites helped identify features to make her art sales website compeitive.
I gathered the research results to create this empathy map that reveals the power of her art story and process as a main user need to inspire an art sale.

Key findings from the research included:

  • Users liked the price of the items and the handmade look of the art, but found it difficult to navigate the existing site
  • They were drawn to Eva's art process and the rich stories behind the artworks
  • Shoppers between 26 and 41 years would highly value online or socials shopping options
  • People prefer to buy from local artists to support them.
  • Quotes about the artwork from buyers and images of Eva with her work are features other artists are using to increase online sales
  • Potential buyers wanted to learn about the each artwork story and the inspiration behind the artwork.

User Personas

Based on our research findings, Eva and I collaborated on two primary user personas representing our target audience segments. These personas helped us empathize with users and guide design decisions throughout the project.

The market persona represents people who buy art at local markets and shows.
The online persona represents people who prefer to shop online.
With our persona's in mind, I created a updated site architecture.
I used the current site architecture and industry standard for art sales websites to help identify this user flow.

Visual Design

To save time and resources, we decided to keep Eva's existing Squarespace platform and customize a new template to align with her brand and user needs. We chose a template together.

As a part of my brief, I offered to also educate Eva on how to manage and update her site on her own after I set it up for her.

We met regularly to brainstorm about the visual design direction, color palette, and typography that would best represent her art style and appeal to her target audience.

The before and after home page for Eva's website with the new template and color branding applied. I used her artist card (see bottom right) to guide colors and fonts.
The final version of the home page with new art sales, customer quotes, features and easy navigation.
The art gallery page allows users to easily browse and filter artworks by category, price, and size. I also helped upload artworks and catagorize them and taught Eva how to maintain and upload new artworks.
The about page shares Eva's art story and process, helping to build a personal connection with potential buyers.

Results & Impact

With the website update complete, we launched the new site and promoted it through Eva's social media channels. I personaly re-visited her art market booth to let her customers know about the new site and helped her share links to a feedback survey to help evaluate usability improvement and build a practice of regular user awareness with Eva.

The response from her existing customers and new visitors was positive, with many praising the improved navigation and visual appeal of the site.

I made sure to regulalry check the site's responsiveness on mobile.

Here are a few quotes from website users and Eva after the redesign:

Overall the website is very clean and easy to use. Looks great!
Thank you so much Sara for re-designing my art website! I had my first online art sale today!

Eva also used the site to help promote a book she and her son wrote and illustrated together after we worked together and the website helped sell their book.

Overall, the project was a success, demonstrating the impact of user-centered design in enhancing online art sales and providing Eva with a platform to grow her art business.
