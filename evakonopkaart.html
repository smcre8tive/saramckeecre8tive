01
Background

The Challenge

Eva Konopka, a talented local artist, wanted to expand her art sales beyond the market booth and into an online presence. Her existing Squarespace site was formatted as a blog — it lacked e-commerce functionality, intuitive navigation, and the visual storytelling that art buyers respond to.

The challenge was to redesign the website within her existing platform to enhance user experience, communicate Eva's artistic identity, and ultimately convert visitors into buyers.

How might we redesign Eva's website so that first-time visitors can discover her story, connect with her art, and purchase a piece — all in one seamless experience?

02
Discovery

Research & Insights

To understand the needs of Eva's potential buyers, I combined secondary research, direct observation at her art market booth, and informal interviews with visitors. I also audited competitor art sales websites to identify best practices and common friction points.

Key findings that shaped the redesign:

  • Users loved the handmade quality and price point, but found the existing site hard to navigate
  • Eva's art process and the stories behind each piece were primary emotional drivers for purchase
  • Shoppers aged 26–41 highly valued online and social shopping options
  • People prefer buying from local artists — supporting the maker matters
  • Buyer quotes and images of Eva with her work were features other artists used to build trust and drive sales
  • Potential buyers wanted to learn the story and inspiration behind each individual artwork

  • Story First

    Buyers connect through Eva's process and inspiration — the story sells the art.

  • Navigation Friction

    The blog format buried artwork — buyers couldn't easily find or filter pieces.

  • Social Proof

    Buyer quotes and photos with Eva build trust for first-time online purchasers.

  • Mobile-Ready

    The 26–41 demographic shops on mobile — responsiveness was non-negotiable.

03
Strategy

Personas & Architecture

Eva and I collaborated on two user personas representing her primary buyer segments. These personas grounded every design decision — from navigation structure to the tone of the about page copy.

04
Design

Visual Design

To save time and resources, we stayed with Eva's existing Squarespace platform and selected a new template together — one that natively supported e-commerce galleries, customer quotes, and full-width imagery. I also committed to teaching Eva how to manage and update the site independently after handoff.

We held regular working sessions to align on visual direction, color palette, and typography — drawing directly from Eva's artist business card to ensure the digital presence felt continuous with her physical brand.

05
Outcomes

Results & Impact

After launch, I promoted the new site at Eva's art market booth in person and helped her distribute a feedback survey to begin building a regular practice of user awareness. The response from existing customers and new visitors was immediate and positive.

"Overall the website is very clean and easy to use. Looks great!"

— Website visitor feedback

"Thank you so much Sara for re-designing my art website! I had my first online art sale today!"

— Eva Konopka, Artist

Beyond the initial launch, the site continued to generate value. Eva used it to promote a children's book she and her son wrote and illustrated together — and the website helped drive those sales as well.

  • First Online Sale

    Eva sold her first artwork online the day the redesigned site launched.

  • User Confidence

    Visitor feedback praised the improved navigation and visual clarity of the site.

  • Lasting Ownership

    Eva now manages updates, adds new artworks, and uses the site as her primary sales channel.

  • Book Sales

    The site went on to help promote and sell a children's book Eva co-wrote with her son.
