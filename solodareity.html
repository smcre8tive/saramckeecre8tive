SoloDareity

Responsive Travel App for Solo Female Travelers

Role: Solo Product Designer | Duration: 2 weeks | Tools: Figma, Lyssna A/B Testing

SoloDareity travel app interface showcasing solo female traveler safety features

Challenge & Problem

An opportunity to design a responsive travel web-app for a specific user poulation in need of support led to this project. Primary and secondary research led to a focus on addressing safety concerns and providing a sense of security for solor female travelers while exploring new destinations abroad.

Solo Female travelers struggle to travel and feel safe with existing support apps for a variety of devices. How might we help solo female travelers feel more secure and confident while traveling abroad using multiple devices?

Research & Discovery

The initial phase focused on understanding solo female traveler user pain points related to solo travel safety and related to use of existing applications. We conducted user interviews, analyzed competitor apps, and conducted primary and secondary product research.

Competitive analysis SWOT matrix comparing key features and user feedback across top travel safety apps

Primary and secondary research confirmed that solo female travelers are a market with significant unmet needs in terms of safety and support. Other competitors do not adequately address these needs.

User Personas

Based on our research findings, I developed three primary user personas representing target audience segments. These personas helped maintain focus on real user needs throughout the design process. Special attention was given towards making personas inclusive and representative of the diversity within the solo female traveler demographic.

Persona 1, Road Trip Ray, prefers road trips and values offline maps and local recommendations for safe routes and accommodations.
Persona 2, Digial Nomad Nat, is a digital nomad who prioritizes reliable wifi and coworking spaces, and seeks community connections for safety and social support.
Persona 3, Introverted Explorer Emory, prefers quiet, off-the-beaten-path destinations and values privacy and personalized safety features.

Research revealed that solo female travelers often feel unsafe in unfamiliar environments and struggle to find reliable wifi support resources. They also expressed a need for apps that are intuitive, secure, and provide real-time safety features.

Information Architecture

Working with the jobs to be done framework, I chose three jobs to be done user flows to guide the information architecture of the app. These flows focused on key user needs such as finding safe accommodations, accessing offline maps, and connecting with local communities for support.

Jobs to be done user flows diagram showing key user tasks and interactions.

Jobs to be done user flows included:

  • When I travel I want to write safety reviews so I can help others that are traveling find locations that are safe.
  • When I travel I want to read safety reviews so I can feel safer as a female solo traveler.
  • When I travel I want to access reliable, downloadable safety maps for chosen areas so I can easily route the safety maps when I am traveling in low-wifi zones.
  • When I travel I want to get travel alerts about trip events to that I do not miss them and end up in an unsafe situation.
    •
    I then consolodated the flows into one streamlined mobile-first wireflow with representative screens for each key user task, ensuring a cohesive and intuitive user experience across the app.

    Low-Fidelity Prototype

    A low-fidelity prototype based on sketched screens allowed us to rapidly test different interface concepts and interaction patterns.

    Low-fidelity wireframe sketches showing multiple mobile app screen layouts for the SoloDareity travel safety application.

    Visual Design & Testing

    For the visual design phase, I focused on creating an accessible and intuitive interface that would support the emotional needs of the solo female traveler while also championing accessibility. The process included creating two mood boards and sharing them with solo female travelers for feedback with various testing methods including asyncronous A/B testing with Lyssna. Then with colors, images and type clarified, the rest of the UI components and responsive screen break-points were created to responsively support multiple screens.

    The Calming style guide, preferred by solo female travelers during testing, defining the visual language and design system for the app.
    The feedback and iterations from the asyncronous A/B user testing sessions, highlighting key insights that informed design refinements and improvements.
    Responsive design mock-ups for mobile, tablet and desktop screens, showing how the interface adapts to different screen sizes.

    Results & Impact

    After prototyping and testing with real users, we created visuals to convey the completed product features for future marketing purposes. Participants in the testing reported feeling more confident and secure with the features, layout and visuals of the final product. A few commented on the value of this product and asked when they would be able to use it for their next trip.

    Mobile access to Location safety reviews written and reviewed by fellow solo female travelers for travel help identify real-time safety concerns.
