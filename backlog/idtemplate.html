E-Learning Course Development

A comprehensive case study documenting the instructional design process for developing an interactive compliance training program for a Fortune 500 financial services company. This project transformed complex regulatory content into engaging, learner-centered modules.

Analysis

The analysis phase involved extensive stakeholder interviews, learner surveys, and content audits. We identified key performance gaps and established clear learning objectives aligned with business goals. Through needs assessment workshops, we discovered that employees struggled with applying compliance knowledge to real-world scenarios, leading us to prioritize scenario-based learning approaches.

Stakeholder interviews revealed critical training gaps and business priorities

Survey data showing learner preferences and current knowledge levels

Visual mapping of performance gaps between current and desired states

Design

During the design phase, we created detailed blueprints for the learning experience. Storyboards mapped out each interaction, while wireframes established the user interface structure. We developed a comprehensive style guide to ensure visual consistency and incorporated microlearning principles to support busy professionals. The instructional strategy emphasized active learning through branching scenarios and formative assessments.

Detailed storyboards outlining content flow and interactions

Low-fidelity wireframes defining navigation and layout structure

Visual style guide establishing color palettes, typography, and brand elements

Hierarchical mapping of learning objectives to course modules

Development

The development phase brought our designs to life using Articulate Storyline 360 and Adobe Creative Suite. We built interactive scenarios with realistic dialogue, created custom graphics and animations, and programmed complex branching logic. Regular reviews with subject matter experts ensured content accuracy, while iterative prototyping allowed us to refine interactions based on early feedback. Accessibility features were integrated from the ground up to ensure WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.

Implementation

Implementation involved deploying the course to the company's learning management system and orchestrating a phased rollout strategy. We conducted train-the-trainer sessions for department leaders, created quick reference guides, and established a support system for technical issues. Change management communications emphasized the value proposition to learners, while gamification elements and completion incentives drove engagement. The pilot group's feedback led to minor refinements before full deployment.

Evaluation

Comprehensive evaluation measured impact at multiple levels using the Kirkpatrick model. Learner satisfaction surveys revealed a 94% approval rating, with participants praising the practical scenarios and clean interface. Knowledge assessments showed a 37% improvement in post-training scores compared to the previous program. Six months post-launch, compliance audit results demonstrated measurable behavior change, with policy violations decreasing by 28%. Business metrics indicated a positive ROI through reduced regulatory risks and improved operational efficiency.
