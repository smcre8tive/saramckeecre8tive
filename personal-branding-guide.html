Personal Brand Guide

Sara McKee | UX/UI Designer

Brand Identity

A clear, compelling personal brand that positions Sara as a thoughtful, accomplished UX/UI designer who prioritizes accessibility and inclusive design.

Core Brand Statement

Sara McKee is a UX/UI designer who believes exceptional design works for everyone. With expertise in inclusive design, accessibility standards, and elegant visual systems, she creates digital experiences that balance aesthetic excellence with functional usability. Her work demonstrates that accessibility and beauty aren't competing values—they're complementary principles that elevate user experiences.

Mission

To create digital experiences that are beautiful, functional, and accessible to all users—proving that inclusive design elevates rather than constrains creativity.

Vision

A digital world where accessibility is the foundation of great design, not an afterthought—where every user can engage with elegant, intuitive interfaces.

Values

Inclusivity, craftsmanship, user empathy, continuous learning, collaboration, and attention to detail in every pixel and interaction.

Interview Talking Points

Key points to emphasize in interviews, networking, and professional conversations.

Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

"I'm Sara McKee, a UX/UI designer specializing in accessible, user-centered digital experiences. I've spent the last 6 years creating WCAG-compliant interfaces that prove accessibility and beauty aren't competing values—they elevate each other. My work ranges from mobile apps to comprehensive design systems, always rooted in user research and inclusive design principles. I'm passionate about creating experiences that work beautifully for everyone."

Why Accessibility Matters to You

"Early in my career, I worked on a project where we retrofitted accessibility features after launch. It was clunky, expensive, and frankly disappointing. That experience taught me that accessibility should be foundational, not an afterthought. Since then, I've seen how accessible design constraints actually push me to create more thoughtful, more elegant solutions. When you design for edge cases, you improve the experience for everyone. That's what excites me about inclusive design—it makes me a better designer."

Handling Design Challenges

"I approach challenges by first deeply understanding the constraints—whether that's technical limitations, tight timelines, or conflicting stakeholder needs. Then I look for creative solutions that satisfy multiple requirements simultaneously. For example, on the IncluDesign project, we needed high contrast for accessibility but didn't want it to feel clinical. The solution was a sophisticated color system with strong contrast ratios but rich, warm tones. It met WCAG standards while maintaining the brand's premium feel."

Communication Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Lead with impact and results
  • Use specific examples and metrics
  • Explain your design decisions
  • Show empathy for user needs
  • Acknowledge team collaboration
  • Share lessons learned
  • Ask thoughtful questions
  • Follow up promptly

✗ Don't

  • Oversell or exaggerate capabilities
  • Dismiss feedback or constraints
  • Get defensive about design choices
  • Use excessive design jargon
  • Bad-mouth previous employers
  • Make promises you can't keep
  • Post controversial opinions
  • Ignore accessibility in examples
